Declaration request: North Queensland Export Terminal at Abbot Point

Status
In progress

On 13 June 2025, a request was made by QCoal Pty Ltd and Byerwen Coal Pty Ltd (QCoal Users), seeking declaration of the coal handling service at the North Queensland Export Terminal at Abbot Point.

We started our investigation into the declaration request on 14 July 2025. Under section 79 of the Queensland Competition Authority Act 1997 Act (QCA Act), we are required to make a recommendation to the Minister on whether to declare the relevant service or part thereof. The relevant Minister is the Minister for Finance, Trade, Employment and Training.

We have published the submissions we received on this request for declaration – initial submissions as well as further submissions in response to those initial submissions.

The published submissions may contain information over which confidentiality has been claimed (under s 187(2) of the QCA Act). We have therefore implemented a confidentiality regime to facilitate access, in appropriate circumstances, to certain material over which confidentiality has been claimed.

Late submission

On 16 December 2025, we published North Queensland Export Terminal Pty Ltd’s submission dated 9 November. Although this information was provided late, we have determined that it is reasonable in all the circumstances to take it into account, in accordance with section 168B of the QCA Act.

We request that any further submissions be provided only after we publish our draft recommendation. Submissions provided outside a designated consultation period will also be subject to review under section 168B of the QCA Act. We may make a recommendation without taking late information into account if we consider doing so is reasonable in all the circumstances.  

We ask that in future the designated submission deadlines are respected by all parties.

All relevant documents are available below.

Section 79A of the QCA Act provides that the QCA must use best endeavours to make a recommendation under section 79 within 6 months (although that period does not include things like consultation periods). The 6-month period is currently due to expire on 21 March 2026. This date will be updated from time to time. The periods that will influence this date, including time allowed for consultation, will only be confirmed later.
